# Digital-literacy-project
This repository contains my Digital Literacy Portfolio, developed in my role as a Student Digital Ambassador. The goal of this project is to demonstrate proficiency in navigating the digital world, from professional branding to online safety and collaborative coding.The project is divided into five modules.


Module 1: Foundational Digital LiteracyCore Objective: Defining the scope and importance of digital literacy in the 21st century.Logic: This task established the "Why." It identified five pillars: Finding Content, Evaluating Information, Using Technology, Sharing Responsibly, and Creating Value.Key Outcome: Developed a "Digital Ambassador Toolkit" focusing on critical thinking and safe online navigation to act as a role model for peers.

Module 2: Professional Digital IdentityCore Objective: Architecting a professional online presence across industry-standard platforms.Logic: This task focused on the "Visibility" of skills. By optimizing LinkedIn, GitHub, ResearchGate, and Kaggle, the transition was made from a student to a professional entity.Key Outcome: Created a synchronized professional brand highlighting expertise in Python, SQL, and Data Analysis, ensuring high-quality engagement with the global tech community.


Module 3: Technical Validation & CollaborationCore Objective: Demonstrating practical coding proficiency and utilizing cloud-based collaboration tools.Logic: This was the "Proof of Competence" phase. It involved passing the HackerRank Intermediate Problem Solving certification to validate algorithmic skills.Key Outcome: Built a Google Forms-based Digital Literacy Quiz to practice data collection and collaborative workflows, bridging the gap between individual skill and community outreach.


Module 4: Professional Communication & EtiquetteCore Objective: Mastering formal digital correspondence and understanding the impact of communication failures.Logic: This task addressed the "Human Element" of technology. It involved drafting professional emails (extension requests and internship inquiries) and analyzing case studies of poor digital communication (e.g., the "Reply All" disaster).Key Outcome: Established a "five-second rule" for digital communication to maintain professional reputation and prevent data leaks or interpersonal conflicts.


Module 5: Cybersecurity & Digital SafetyCore Objective: Analyzing modern cyber threats and implementing personal security protocols.Logic: This final task was the "Defense" layer. It involved a deep dive into Phishing and UPI Fraud, specifically focusing on how social engineering targets college students.



Key Outcome: Performed a personal security audit, moving social profiles to private and implementing strict verification rules for urgent digital requests to mitigate the risks of vishing and deepfakes.Conclusion of the Logic ChainThe progression of these five PDFs follows a logical path: Understand (Task 1) $\rightarrow$ Represent (Task 2) $\rightarrow$ Execute (Task 3) $\rightarrow$ Communicate (Task 4) $\rightarrow$ Protect (Task 5)
